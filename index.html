About

Who
I Am

I am a dual degree student at IIIT Hyderabad, pursuing a B.Tech in Computer Science along with an MS in Computational Natural Sciences. I qualified for the Indian National Mathematical Olympiad in 2022 among the ~300 students selected nationally and attended the INMO Training Camp at ICTS-TIFR, Bengaluru.

I work as an undergraduate researcher at the CCNSB lab under Prof. Chittaranjan Hens, where I study complex networks, synchronization, and phase transitions in dynamical systems. My work involves modeling large-scale network behaviour, structural balance in signed graphs, and connectivity collapse in networked systems.

Alongside research, I build in two areas: quantitative finance, where I work on derivatives pricing, market microstructure, and statistical modeling of markets, and systems software, where I focus on performance-critical and low-level implementation.

Outside academics, I am a national-level swimmer, and I play table tennis competitively. I am deeply interested in the engineering of superbikes and enjoy studying their mechanics, aerodynamics, and performance limits.
