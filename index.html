About

Who
I Am

I am a second year dual degree student at IIIT Hyderabad, pursuing a B.Tech in Computer Science along with an MS in Computational Natural Sciences. I qualified for the Indian National Mathematical Olympiad in 2022 among the ~300 students selected nationally and attended the INMO Training Camp at ICTS TIFR.

I work as an undergraduate researcher at the CCNSB lab under Prof. Chittaranjan Hens, where I study complex networks, synchronization, and phase transitions in dynamical systems. My work involves modeling large scale network behaviour, structural balance in signed graphs, and connectivity collapse in networked systems.

Outside academics, I am a national level swimmer and I play table tennis competitively. I am deeply interested in the engineering of superbikes and enjoy studying their mechanics, aerodynamics, and performance limits. I like disciplines that require precision, control, and analytical thinking.
