Flash Crash Dynamics in Networked Oscillators

CCNSB Lab, IIIT Hyderabad · Advisor: Prof. Chittaranjan Hens
VIEW CODE →

What It Does

A simulation framework models financial flash crashes as phase transitions in coupled oscillator networks. The ODE integration runs in a C++ RK4 engine, which keeps large scale parameter sweeps tractable. Recovers the theoretical critical coupling to within 2% across 525 simulations.

It treats a synchronized market as a network of Kuramoto and Stuart-Landau oscillators, where a flash crash is an abrupt drop in coupling. The question it asks is whether the network recovers coherence after such a drop and how fast.

How It Works

A C++ Integration Engine

The core ODE integration loop runs in C++ with a fixed step RK4 engine over sparse adjacency lists. A single simulation of 400 oscillators runs in about 0.15 seconds, which brings the full parameter sweep down to about 80 seconds. Sparse lists hold memory and access time to linear rather than quadratic.

Two Models, Two Topologies

Kuramoto oscillators track phase, while Stuart-Landau oscillators track complex amplitude and capture amplitude death. The framework compares Erdos-Renyi and Barabasi-Albert networks. Coherence collapses gradually on ER networks because no hubs anchor synchrony, while hubs hold synchrony longer on BA networks and then fail faster and more completely.

Critical Coupling and Early Warnings

A finite size scaling analysis over 525 simulations, across 5 system sizes and 21 coupling values, recovers the theoretical critical coupling to within 2%. Rolling variance and autocorrelation both rise in the window before the transition, the critical slowing down precursor that market microstructure researchers see before real flash crashes.

Results

The C++ engine completes the full sweep in about 80 seconds. The empirical steady state order parameter converges to within 2% of the theoretical critical coupling across 525 simulations, and the phase diagram locates the transition boundary sharply.
